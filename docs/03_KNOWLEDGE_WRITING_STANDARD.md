**Status:** LOCKED

---

# Purpose

Every optimization in TweakMind is first a piece of knowledge.

Only after users understand the knowledge should optimization become available.

This document ensures that every optimization follows the same quality standard.

Consistency is more important than quantity.

---

# Core Principle

Optimization knowledge exists to support decision making.

It does not exist to persuade users to optimize.

It does not exist to maximize optimization count.

Its purpose is to help users understand whether an optimization is valuable for their own situation.

---

# Writing Rules

## Rule 1

Always explain:

**What it is.**

Never assume prior technical knowledge.

---

## Rule 2

Always explain:

**Why Microsoft designed it this way.**

Understanding the original design always comes before discussing optimization.

---

## Rule 3

Only after explaining Microsoft's design should the document explain:

**Why some users choose to optimize it.**

Optimization is a choice,

not the default.

---

## Rule 4

Never use vague statements.

Avoid wording such as:

- improves performance

- boosts speed

- makes Windows faster

- optimized

- enhanced

Instead,

describe concrete effects.

Examples:

- reduces background disk activity

- decreases memory usage

- reduces indexing workload

- changes search behavior

---

## Rule 5

Every optimization must explain both:

- Benefits

- Trade-offs

Trade-offs are mandatory.

Knowledge without trade-offs is incomplete.

---

## Rule 6

Every optimization must clearly identify:

- suitable users

- unsuitable users

Recommendations are contextual.

There is no universal recommendation.

---

## Rule 7

Recovery information is mandatory.

Recovery should be explained before users apply an optimization.

Users should know how to undo every change.

---

## Rule 8

Risk descriptions should educate.

Warnings should explain:

- what may happen

- why it may happen

- who may be affected

Warnings should never create unnecessary fear.

---

## Rule 9

Knowledge should describe facts.

AI may generate recommendations,

but knowledge itself should remain objective.

Facts never change.

Recommendations may change.

---

## Rule 10

Terminology follows the three official terminology modes.

- Original

- Localized

- TweakMind

Terminology changes presentation,

never meaning.

---

# Language Style

Optimization knowledge should be:

- clear

- neutral

- technically accurate

- educational

- concise

Avoid:

- marketing language

- exaggerated claims

- emotional wording

- unnecessary technical jargon

---

# Evidence Standard

Every optimization should be supported by reliable evidence.

Evidence may include:

- Microsoft documentation

- official Windows behavior

- verified technical analysis

- repeatable testing

- widely validated community findings

Assumptions should never be presented as facts.

---

# Recommendation Principle

Recommendations are generated using:

- user goals

- hardware

- Windows version

- optimization knowledge

Recommendations should always explain:

- why

- expected value

- possible downside

The final decision always belongs to the user.

---

# Quality Checklist

Before an optimization is accepted into TweakMind,

confirm that it answers all of the following questions.

- What is it?

- Why does Microsoft provide it?

- Why do people optimize it?

- What changes?

- What are the benefits?

- What are the trade-offs?

- Who should use it?

- Who should avoid it?

- What are the risks?

- How can it be recovered?

If any answer is missing,

the optimization knowledge is incomplete.

---

# Completion Standard

Optimization knowledge is considered complete only when users can confidently answer:

- Do I understand this optimization?

- Is it valuable for me?

- What will I gain?

- What will I lose?

- Can I recover if necessary?

If users still need external tutorials,

the knowledge is not complete.

---

# One Sentence

Optimization knowledge should never tell users what to do.

It should give users enough understanding to decide for themselves.
